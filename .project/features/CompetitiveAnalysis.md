### Proloquo2Go Deep Dive

**Vocabulary System (Crescendo)**:
- 4,750 unique words directly accessible + 7,250 pre-categorized in storage
- Claims 4x-7x more words than competitors at smaller grid sizes
- 3 vocabulary levels: Basic, Intermediate Core, Advanced Core
- Also supports Gateway to Language & Learning vocabularies
- Core words on home page, repeated in same position on fringe pages (motor planning)
- Fringe words organized by semantic association (e.g., "go" folder has drive, explore, fly, ride, walk)
- Activity templates keep core words visible even in activity-specific pages

**Grid System**:
- 23 different grid sizes (9 to 144 buttons per page)
- Per-folder grid size can be changed independently
- Research showed 77 buttons/page achieved same outcomes as other configs

**Symbol System**:
- 20,000+ SymbolStix symbols included (no PCS support)
- Users can add own photos

**Voices**:
- 100+ free natural-sounding voices (Acapela Neural)
- Languages: English (US/UK/AU), Spanish, French, Dutch
- Apple Personal Voice support
- Speed and pitch adjustable

**Grammar Engine**:
- Full verb conjugation/inflection
- Noun, pronoun, adjective inflection
- Progression from single words to complex sentences

**Color Coding**:
- Modified Fitzgerald Key (same system we use)
- Verbs=green, nouns=orange, descriptors=blue, pronouns=yellow, social=pink, prepositions=purple
- Customizable schemes

**Progressive Language**:
- Hide buttons without changing grid size
- Reveal in developmental order
- Button positions remain fixed (motor planning consistency)

**Access Methods**:
- Direct touch, eye tracking (iOS 18 native + Hiru/TD Pilot/VersaEye)
- Head tracking (HeadMouse Nano)
- Switch scanning (single/two-switch, multiple modes)
- Apple Watch as input device
- External Bluetooth keyboard

**Multi-Language**:
- English, Spanish, French, Dutch
- Bilingual Spanish/English (switch mid-sentence)

**Backup**:
- Automatic daily backups
- Cloud backup to iCloud, Dropbox, Google Drive

### Proloquo (Next-Gen) Deep Dive

**Key Differences from Proloquo2Go**:
- Subscription model (free download, 1-month free trial, 50% off first year)
- Fixed 60-button grid (by design -- optimized motor planning)
- English only (major limitation)
- Up to 16,000 words for adult users (5-6x more than competitors claim)
- Cross-device sync included

**Unique Features**:
- **Thinking Space**: Flexible area to arrange symbols freely, supports non-linear expression
- **Related Words**: Built-in thesaurus for more specific/nuanced words
- **Proloquo Coach**: Companion training app for families/educators (free with subscription)
- Can be shared with teachers, SLPs, grandparents for free

**Direct License Purchases** (outside App Store, for insurance claims):
- $99.99 (1 year) to $499.99 (5 years)

### Education and Professional Pricing

- Regional partners: DTSL (UK), AccessATCanada, Communication Matters (UK)

## 6. User Pain Points with Proloquo2Go

Aggregated from reviews, forums, and Reddit:

**Cost barrier**: $249.99 + iPad ($300-500+) = $550-750+ total entry cost. No Android option forces families into Apple ecosystem.

**Steep learning curve**: Described as "big learning curve in navigation." Customization is "very difficult and frustrating." Requires 6-part webinar series to train professionals. Not recommended for first-time AAC users.

**Confusing UX/Navigation**: Described as "messy" and "more confusing to children." Finding words requires navigating multiple button layers. Vocabulary positions shift when grid sizes change, disorienting users.

**Voice quality issues**: Recent updates added "grating breathy quality" and "weird blip sounds."

**Platform lock-in**: iOS-only. No way to use across mixed-device households. Proprietary vocabulary format means heavy switching costs.

**Subscription backlash**: Proloquo's subscription model incompatible with state funding programs. Strong resistance from families and funding agencies.

## 7. Gaps and Opportunities

### Gap 1: Cross-Platform Access -- MASSIVE
Nearly all premium AAC apps are iOS-only. Android users and mixed-device families are stranded. A polished web-based PWA fills a gaping hole.

### Gap 2: Affordability and Funding Compatibility
Premium apps cost $150-$300+ plus iPad cost. Subscription models incompatible with Medicaid/state funding. Free alternatives lack depth. A low-cost one-time purchase that works on any device serves millions of underserved families.

### Gap 3: Modern UX and Ease of Use
Legacy apps have steep learning curves. Parents struggle to customize without SLP training. Interfaces feel dated. Modern UX with simple onboarding and sensible defaults is a major differentiator.

### Gap 4: Caregiver/Therapist Collaboration
Most apps are single-user, single-device. Built-in collaboration (shared boards, remote editing, progress sharing) is high-value. CoughDrop leads here with team accounts.

### Gap 5: Adult Users and Acquired Conditions
Most apps target children. Adults with aphasia, ALS, Parkinson's, TBI are underserved. Huge population.

### Gap 6: Multilingual and Global Access
Most systems are English-first with translated boards. Core word research in non-English languages is limited. Culturally appropriate (not just translated) AAC is a major need. Cboard covers 40+ languages but lacks depth.

### Gap 7: AI-Powered Communication
AI features are nascent across the market. Context-aware predictions, conversational assistance, and personalized learning are the next frontier. Only Vocable (ChatGPT) and Spoken (pattern learning) have started here.

### Gap 8: Data Portability
Proprietary formats create vendor lock-in. Open Board Format (OBF) exists but adoption is limited. Supporting open formats reduces switching anxiety.

### Gap 9: No Data/Analytics
No major competitor offers meaningful usage tracking, progress reports, or caregiver insights. AssistiveWare has zero analytics features. We already have Data and Usage Tracking planned.

## 9. Donation Panel and Community Features (Website)

### Why a Donation Panel

Given our $9.99 price point (deliberately low to maximize access), a donation system lets supporters who believe in the mission contribute beyond the purchase price. This is especially compelling because:

- AAC is a social cause (communication is a human right)
- Families who can afford more can subsidize access for those who cannot
- Feature requests tied to donations create community-driven development
- Transparency about funding builds trust

### Recommended Donation Panel Features

**Core Components**:
- Direct donation via Stripe/PayPal (one-time and recurring)
- "Sponsor a Family" option -- donate a license for a family in need
- Transparent funding tracker showing what donations support
- Feature request board where donors can submit and upvote ideas

**Feature Request System**:
- Public board showing requested features with vote counts
- Status labels: Requested, Planned, In Progress, Shipped
- Users can submit ideas with plain-language descriptions
- Voting system to surface highest-demand features

**Trust and Transparency**:
- Show how funds are allocated (development, hosting, accessibility testing)
- Public roadmap tied to community input
- Regular updates on progress

**Community Engagement**:
- Testimonials from families (with consent)
- "Wall of supporters" for donors who opt in
- Newsletter signup for development updates
- Social sharing for advocacy
