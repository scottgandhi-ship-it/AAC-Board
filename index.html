Getting Started

Tap words on the board while you talk out loud. Say "I want juice" while tapping each word. This is called modeling, and it's the single most powerful thing you can do. Your child is watching and learning even when it doesn't look like it.

Never say "show me where juice is" or "use your words." That turns communication into a test. Instead, just tap words during meals, bath time, play, and bedtime. The more natural it feels, the faster your child connects with it.

If they pull your hand, grunt, point, or tap just one word -- that's communication. Celebrate it. The board doesn't replace what they're already doing. It gives them another way to be heard.

If your child isn't using the board yet, you model single words: "want." "more." "help." If they're tapping one word, you model two: "want juice." Always just one step ahead. Never three.

These core words get used dozens of times every day in every situation. They matter way more than specific nouns. Your child will build everything on top of these.

Processing takes time, especially for young communicators. Most of us jump in after 2 seconds -- that's not enough. Pause, wait, and trust that something is happening even in the silence.

The more places your child sees the board being used, the faster it becomes part of their world. Keep it nearby and pull it into as many moments as you can throughout the day.

That is completely normal. You are not failing. Every time you model a word, you're planting a seed. Some days it will feel like nothing is happening. Keep going. You are giving your child a voice, and that is extraordinary.
